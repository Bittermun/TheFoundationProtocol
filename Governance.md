# TFP Foundation Protocol — Governance Charter
**Version 1.1 (Pre-Mainnet)**
**Date: April 2026**
**Status: Active**

---

## Preamble

TFP is a mutualistic digital commons: uncensorable, discoverable, user-publishable, and self-archiving.

In its fragile early phase, the protocol operates under temporary extreme founder stewardship with strong, bounded backstop powers. These powers exist solely to protect the core vision while the network is small and unproven. The founder commits to voluntarily surrendering them as the commons demonstrates real resilience and maturity. This design is deliberate: extreme early protection followed by graceful, community-supported handover creates the strongest possible foundation for long-term mutualism.

Every exercise of power under this charter is publicly logged, contestable, and time-bound. No permanent central authority is created or intended.

TFP is designed to protect during periods of civilizational disruption
---

## 1. Core Principles

- **Mutualistic and restorative.** Rewards contribution; repairs harm through reputation, transparency, and voluntary cooperation rather than punishment.
- **Expertise-first.** Decisions flow to those with verified relevant knowledge for the specific issue at hand.
- **Transparent by default.** All votes, escalations, bench actions, cohort outputs, defensive reports, and founder power exercises are publicly logged with written justification.
- **Progressive decentralization.** Extreme founder stewardship is temporary by explicit design; authority shifts voluntarily to the community at earned milestones.
- **Cooperative defense.** The system protects itself through alliances, transparent reporting, and collective will — never through hidden control or monetary capture.

## 2. Governance Bodies

### 2.1 Steering Committee (3–5 seats)

Handles Tier 2 (high-stakes) matters only. Has no authority over Tier 1 decisions.

**Founder seat**
Permanent during the stewardship phase. Holds extreme but bounded backstop powers (see Section 4). Excluded from confirmation votes on expert seats to prevent circular self-selection.

**Expert seats (2–4)**
Filled from the Expert Bench via the following defined process:

1. Open nomination call to the full Bench (minimum 7-day window).
2. Candidates ranked by verified reputation score (credit ledger + verifiable contribution history).
3. Top-ranked candidates fill available seats via Bench confirmation vote (simple majority, founder excluded).
4. Founder may veto one confirmed candidate per seat per term. Veto must be filed publicly within 72 hours with written justification.
5. A unanimous Bench vote (excluding founder) may override the veto. Override is logged publicly.
6. Term: 12 months, renewable once consecutively before a mandatory one-term break.

Voting on the committee is one seat, one vote. Reputation weighting determines who reaches the committee — not how members vote once seated.

---

### 2.2 Expert Bench (8–12 person pool)

The primary working body for Tier 1 decisions and the sole source of Steering Committee members.

**Joining:**
- Open application reviewed by existing Bench (simple majority approval).
- Founder acknowledgment required within 14 days; if not exercised, candidate is auto-approved. This is acknowledgment only — not a veto — to prevent silent blocking of Bench growth.
- Criteria: verifiable domain expertise, demonstrated contribution to TFP or aligned commons projects, no undisclosed conflicts of interest (self-declared and ledger-verifiable).

**Issue assignment:**
Relevant Bench members self-nominate for each matter. If fewer than three self-nominate within 48 hours of posting, the Steering Committee may assign directly. All assignments are public and logged.

**Bench standing:**
Members serve indefinitely unless removed for a defined charter breach (Section 4). Rotation in and out of issue assignments does not affect Bench standing.

---

### 2.3 Public Observer Seat (1 seat, advisory only)

Filled every 3–6 months by the highest-reputation verified user (by credit ledger) who has not previously held the seat, opts in during the selection window, and is confirmed eligible by simple Bench majority. Bench confirmation is limited to verifying eligibility criteria — it is not a discretionary veto. Zero voting power. May submit written commentary on any decision; commentary is permanently logged alongside the relevant decision record.

---

### 2.4 Communication and Optics

**Anonymous Pitch Channel**
Any governance member, including the founder, may submit proposals, thoughts, or veto justifications via a logged, on-chain anonymous form. Anonymity protects the identity of the submitter — not the content, which is always logged in full. The Bench may refine language before public posting; original authorship is disclosed in the internal log and may be voluntarily attributed in the public record by the submitter.

**Optional Proxy Speaker**
Any governance member may designate a rotating "Vision Steward Proxy" from the Bench to articulate ideas in meetings or public logs. Original authorship is credited in the final record if desired. The proxy role rotates to prevent it from becoming a new power center.

Both mechanisms are opt-in and fully transparent in their operation.

---

### 2.5 Reputational Cohorts (Deferred Motivation Layer)

Small, time-bound groups may be formed in the future for concrete work (heuristic reviews, plugin vetting, compliance drafting, etc.) once the network has sufficient size and prestige to make participation meaningful. No cohort requirement or formal structure is institutionalized in this charter. Details — including formation, recognition formats, mentorship protocols, and legacy practices — will live in a separately maintained, community-editable Cohort Handbook published post-testbed.

## 3. Decision Tiers and Escalation

**Tier 1 — Routine Operations**
Handled by the Expert Bench, with cohorts assisting where applicable. Examples: heuristic parameter updates, plugin approvals, minor documentation changes, bench assignment decisions.

**Escalation to Tier 2:** Any Bench member, Steering Committee member, or the founder may flag a Tier 1 matter as Tier 2 with written reasoning. Once flagged, the matter automatically escalates and cannot proceed as Tier 1 without a formal de-escalation vote.

**De-escalation:** Requires simple majority Steering Committee vote, publicly logged.

When a Bench majority declares an active emergency, standard onboarding and alliance approval timelines compress to 48 hours. All decisions made under fast-track are subject to full retrospective review at the next regular Tier 2 session, publicly logged with justification for the compression. Fast-track status expires automatically after 30 days unless renewed by the same process.

---

**Tier 2 — High-Stakes Decisions**
Requires full Steering Committee deliberation and explicit founder approval. Examples: core protocol changes, major moderation policy, any monetization proposal, regulatory positioning, charter amendments, member removals, and defense alliance approvals.

All Tier 2 decisions are published with full reasoning before taking effect. Minimum 72-hour public comment window before any vote. Public Observer commentary is included in the record.

## 4. Founder Powers — Extreme, Bounded, and Temporary

### Veto
Applies to Tier 2 decisions only. Must be exercised within 72 hours of committee vote with written public justification. May be overridden by unanimous Steering Committee vote (excluding founder seat); override is logged and published.

### Emergency Pause
Halts a specific decision or action — not the entire network. Duration: 48–72 hours pending review. Must be publicly logged within 24 hours. Cannot be applied to the same decision more than once.

### Replacement Authority
The founder may remove a Steering Committee or Bench member under the following defined grounds only:

- Documented, verified conflict of interest not previously disclosed.
- Sustained absence: missing three or more consecutive required votes or assignments without advance notice filed before the absence begins.
- Verifiable external financial or political influence on governance activity.
- Documented, repeated violation of this charter.

Policy disagreement alone is not valid grounds for removal.

**Process:** 30-day public notice with written justification citing the specific ground above. Affected member may submit a public response within that window, logged alongside the justification. Replacement follows the standard seat-filling process in Section 2.1 or 2.2 as applicable.

---

### Voluntary Surrender

The founder may at any time publicly and permanently surrender any or all powers listed in this section. Surrender is irrevocable: once filed and publicly logged, surrendered powers transfer permanently to the Steering Committee by default unless the surrender document specifies otherwise. No single individual inherits surrendered powers. Surrender is encouraged once clear maturity signals are present (sustained testbed stability, successful defensive transparency reports, meaningful active-user growth) and is framed as a deliberate, high-visibility act of trust in the commons.

---

### Emergency Steward Mode (Cooperative Reinstatement)

In a genuine crisis — coordinated external attack, mass bribery or blackmail of governance members, or existential threat to the uncensorable core — the Expert Bench may formally request temporary resumption of backstop powers.

**If the founder has not yet surrendered powers:**
1. Documented evidence of the crisis is submitted to the full Bench.
2. Simple majority Bench vote formally requests reinstatement.
3. Founder retains the absolute right to accept or refuse.
4. If accepted, powers are strictly scope-limited, time-bound (default 14 days), and expire automatically unless renewed by the same process.
5. All requests, responses, decisions, and outcomes are permanently logged.

**If the founder has already surrendered powers:**
The Expert Bench may submit a documented crisis request to the founder. The founder retains an absolute right to accept or refuse. If accepted, powers are strictly scope-limited, time-bound (default 14 days), and expire automatically unless renewed by identical process. No override mechanism exists to compel acceptance. If refused, the Steering Committee may enact narrow, crisis-specific containment measures under Tier 2 procedures — but surrendered stewardship powers are not restorable by any vote or mechanism. This preserves voluntary surrender as a permanent, unconditional trust signal while allowing cooperative crisis response.

## 5. Defensive Capabilities

The commons defends itself through mutualistic, win-win mechanisms rather than punitive or centralized tools. No internal compute quotas, fixed resource pools, or penalization mechanisms are created under this section.

### Mutualistic Defense Alliances
The Steering Committee may approve opt-in partnerships with external allies (NGOs, researchers, friendly protocols) who volunteer compute, bandwidth, or expertise for defensive work — heuristic hardening, attack simulation, resilience testing. In return, partners receive early access or prestige recognition.

All alliances must be explicitly opt-in, resource-volunteering, and philosophically aligned with TFP's uncensorable, mutualistic core. No financial dependencies, data-sharing mandates, or governance influence are permitted under any partnership. All partnership terms are publicly logged. Active alliances are capped at five at any time; each is time-limited to 12-month terms and renewable only by Tier 2 vote.

### Defensive Transparency Reports
The Bench produces regular public reports — quarterly at minimum, or immediately following a detected attack or coercion attempt — documenting evidence of hacking probes, influence campaigns, or blackmail attempts, and how the system resisted locally via heuristics, local caches, and ZK proofs. No accusations are made; only documented evidence and system responses. Reports are permanently archived and publicly accessible. They serve as the primary reputational shield and deterrent.

---

## 6. Versioning and Amendment

This charter is version-controlled in the public repository. All amendments require Tier 2 approval and are logged on-chain where protocol infrastructure supports it. Prior versions remain permanently and publicly accessible.

No amendment may remove the public logging requirement or the defined grounds for founder powers.

**Foundational Ethos Anchor:** No amendment may alter the temporary nature of founder stewardship, the irrevocability of voluntary surrender, or the mutualistic and non-punitive defense posture established in this charter. These principles constitute TFP's immutable social contract and survive all future revisions.
